Hamburgers

Description

Who doesn't love a good hamburger? The fluffy buns, the juicy meat, and whatever toppings your heart desires - sounds wonderful! This American staple is a delicious meal that is easy on the budget as well as your time.

Traditionally hamburgers are made with beef, but don't let that restrict you. I have had some delicious pork burgers, and even had some pork/beef mixed burgers. But you can enjoy a turkey burger or a chicken burger too! Just make sure that you cook the burgers to the accurate temperature for the meat you are using. Beef can still be a little pink, but I recommend all others are cooked well done.

Toppings for a burger can be whatever your heart desires. Some enjoy a plain burger. Others like them traditionally decked out with ketchup, mustard, mayo, pickles, onion, and lettuce. I have heard of some adventurous folks even putting a fried egg or peanut butter on their burgers!

The recipe below is one that I use regulary to make burgers at home. This recipe works great when either grilling or pan frying. I find that the Worcestershire sauce gives it a great flavor without needing to add extra salt and the pepper puts a nice kick in the burger. I hope you enjoy these as much as I do.

Also I don't usually measure out the ingredients, I just eyeball it. Feel free to add more or less to your taste.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef - 73/27 does great because you need the fat for flavor and so the burger will stick together
  • 2 tbsp black pepper
  • 1/4 cup Worcestershire sauce
  • Hamburger buns
  • Desired toppings

Steps

  1. Start by placing the ground beef (or whatever ground meat you chose) into a large mixing bowl. Then add the pepper and Worcestershire sauce. Mix the ingredients thoroughly using clean hands or a large wooden spoon.
  2. Next divide the ground beef into 4 even sized balls. This will give you roughly quarter pound sized patties. (You don't have to be exact in this.) Then flatten them out into a patty shape of roughly 1/2 inch thickness.
  3. This step is optional, but if the meat is feeling very sticky and wanting to fall apart, place them on some parchment paper on a baking sheet. Put the baking sheet into the freezer for about 15 minutes to firm them up.
  4. Place a large skillet on the stovetop on medium high heat. Fit as many burgers as you can without crowding. Cook them to the desired pinkness. If you have a meat thermometer, check them off the heat to make sure they are reading an internal temp of at least 160 degree F.
  5. Once cooked, let the meat rest while you assemble the toppings on your buns. Then enjoy!
